You know those things you do a certain way because it's how you've always done them?

One of those things for me is the route I take when I walk my dog, Paulie. Out of habit, we always make a left when we leave our apartment.

Actually, that's kind of a lie.

There's also a good reason for our habit of walking left. If you leave the building and make a right you have to squeeze by a grocery store called The Yellow Basket, whose produce storage room doubles as a public sidewalk.

Publicly exposed produce is enough of a reason to walk the other way, if you ask me.

But one day in March, 2021, Paulie and I decided to brave the produce and walk in a bold new direction.

We took a right out of our apartment, successfully traversed the tomato obstacle course, and were on our way toward the water.

It was like being in an entirely new neighborhood. After more than two years of living in the same apartment, I never once turned left and walked to the water on Division Ave.
